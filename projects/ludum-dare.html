Goal and Explanation

Game jams were always something I felt a little intimidated by. With a short amount of time, you are expected to create a complete game with a gameplay loop, passable visuals and audio, playable, and bug free. And most of all, going through the motions of game development within such a short time can get very stressful!

Ludum Dare is a jam which encourages collaboration, so it comes natural that I've had to work with people. I've teamed up with several friends of mine throughout these game jams. Notable partners include my good friends Bobbie, Rabbit, and Auros, however I've worked with several other friends over the course of my game jam adventure.

Currently, I've exclusively used the Unity engine to create my games. I am the most familiar and most productive in Unity, however considerations have been given to Godot for future projects.

Problems and Growing Pains

Having not participated in any game jam until Ludum Dare, I had to learn time management and project scoping very quickly. Fortunately, my partners in crime were similarly inexperienced in game jams, so it was a collaborative learning process for all of us.

With each Ludum Dare game jam came similar and unique challenges to overcome. We've had projects suffer from over-scoping, projects suffer from not having enough people, projects suffer from having too many people, and projects suffering from the lack of a dedicated 3D artist, VFX, or composer.

Lessons Learned

With each Ludum Dare game jam I've participated in, the more I learn about the true game development process, and the more refined I become for future jams.

These jams have allowed me to learn small team communication and collaboration, properly managing my time and scoping my projects, and working under time crunches to push a finished product.

These lessons have allowed me to produce higher quality games, including some that have ranked fairly highly.
