ChroMapper

ChroMapper is a Unity application designed for creating maps for the popular rhythm game Beat Saber.

This was created from scratch, with help from other developers in the open source community.

Goal and Explanation

ChroMapper was created out of a need of an improved map editor for the Beat Saber community. At the time, the existing map editors had some areas of improvement that I wanted to rectify. This included general usability, and the lack of new features.

In February of 2019, a friend and I started work on ChroMapper. We chose Unity as our engine for a few reasons: First, it would match the engine used by Beat Saber, and second, it was the engine both of us were the most familiar in.

Problems and Growing Pains

Over the course of ChroMapper's development, I ran into multiple issues that turned into large learning experiences. I never knew how large the project would become, so I quickly learned the importance of organizing and maintaining code.

I quickly learned and applied object-oriented programming concepts, such as interfaces and abstract classes, to reuse as much common functionality as possible, while also allowing for slight variations in behavior.

Lessons Learned

ChroMapper is easily the largest project I've worked on, and the challenges I faced certainly morphed me into a much better developer.

While project structure and maintainence were big lessons I learned, the best lessons that come out of developing ChroMapper were in the optimization department. Multiple times in development, I had to sit down and rewrite systems that caused poor performance; from implementing object pools, to optimizing rendering performance, and even making my own simple (yet fast) raycast system.
